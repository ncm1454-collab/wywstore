AI Product Recommendations That Actually Fit

Learn how to get AI product recommendations based on your needs, constraints, and trade-offs.

In this article

1. A recommendation is only as personal as the input 2. Ask for a small, diverse shortlist 3. Demand reasons tied to your request 4. Make compromises explicit 5. Verify before buying

A recommendation should feel like the end of a chain of reasoning, not a product name dropped from the sky. Whenever AI recommends something, I want to know what it understood, which compromises it made, and why this option survived.

A recommendation is only as personal as the input

Generic requests produce generic winners. Add how often you will use the product, where it will be stored, who else will use it, and what disappointed you about previous purchases.

Ask for a small, diverse shortlist

Three or four distinct options are usually more useful than fifteen similar ones. Ask for a best overall choice, a value choice, and an alternative that solves the problem differently.

Demand reasons tied to your request

The explanation should repeat your actual priorities, not marketing claims. If quiet operation mattered, the recommendation should say how the product addresses noise.

Make compromises explicit

Every recommendation gives something up. Ask what you sacrifice by choosing each option. This prevents the polished summary from hiding a painful weakness.

Verify before buying

Use AI to reach a confident shortlist, then confirm current specifications, availability, and seller conditions. Good recommendations accelerate judgment; they do not replace it.

Try a request like this

“Recommend three office chairs under €450 for someone 178 cm tall who sits eight hours per day, gets warm easily, and wants adjustable armrests. Avoid bonded leather and overly aggressive lumbar support.”
